Hi everyone! My name is Judy. A few years ago, I moved to Israel to join the IDF. I was an F-16 technician in a very operational squadron and it was one of the hardest yet most fulfilling things I've ever done in my life. I am currently a veteran and I am about to start university here at Tel Aviv University.

Most released soldiers are able to save money and travel the world, by staying with their parents. I on the other hand have been working my butt off just to survive. My parents currently pay for my two younger brothers' tuitions so they don't have much to support me, as well. My dream is to be able to travel and to study without having to worry how I'm going to pay this month's rent and bills (and help my parents out!). It would mean the world to me if you could help! Thank you!

My beloved Judy. As I see I'm the only one who is participating I'm going to use this as a private platform. And write to you from the bottom of my heart. Firstly, if taking a trip is your hearts desire, and you struggle, I'm always here to help you. At the same time, I have to say the texts I read here are baffling to me. The girl I see in that photo is the person I met, and grew to care for dearly. She is responsible, loving, smart, independent, able, with a bright bright future ahead. I really worry for her and miss her. I see something is up with her and would love to pull her back. Feel like I can't. That resourceful girl might have set up a page like this, to seek recognition and support from the community. What she has done for her self and the country is commendable, and deserves every bit of support and recognition. At the same time, she would never put her parents in that kind of position. You have just come back from a vacation with them all throughout June. On that vacation you took a long cruise together, not exactly the kind of parents who need you to 'support them financially'. When you came back from that vacation, you took an extra week off to be with your family that was visiting, another week off to move an apartment during the week (which eventually didn't happen) and another week where you were under the weather. After that, most days your signed up for a full day at work, you seek replacement mid-day and leave, and it takes a lot of encouragement to get you to show up even for those half days. Life is not a rose-garden. You know that. If you look to see what hard work looks like, you have great examples, I land at 6AM from a flight, then off to work by 9AM. I cannot afford to be sick or miss work. For the same reason. I struggle with bills, and life and my responsibilities towards other. The Judy I met knew all about that. Today's Judy struggles. And doesn't necessarily fight as hard. This mini-site is an extension of that for its text and approach. I don't write this to hurt your feelings. I write this to put it in front of you. All the success and good things you are destined for are at risk, when they lay on a such foundations. You are so great, so good, so smart. I had challenges to at that period of my life. How you deal with it will make you or break you. I make this donation to your cause with all my heart. And write to you and the Judy in that photo with admiration. Yours ever, Yoni.
